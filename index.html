I'm Joey

I'm a lifelong learner, and prior to returning to school to study Computer Science, I had the privilege of traveling the country and making music. To support myself, I worked in the restaurant industry for almost 6 years, before finally deciding to pivot to what I knew in my heart was where my skills and passions aligned.

Since resuming my studies at the University of Washington, I've taken classes that have rigorously challenged my reasoning and problem-solving skills in ways I couldn't have imagined. I've also had the opportunity to teach students just starting their tech journey, the same one I had the privilege of starting back in January of 2022 when I wrote my first line of code.

My favorite part of software development is building things. I've been building app designs and coming up with project ideas long before I started coding. The only difference now is that I have the skills to build them! In October 2022, three of my fellow transfer students and I built an application that translates novels into AI-generated picture books in the DubHacks hackathon and placed as finalists amongst 455 contestants. In April of the following year, we participated in the Dempsey Startup Competition, where we designed an application that translates a speaker's voice in a video to another language recreated on a vocal model of the speaker. In doing so, we had the opportunity to present and get advice from hundreds of judges about the business decisions it takes to get a startup off the ground.

As an engineer, I'm always open to talking with people interested in the latest technology, ideas, and design patterns. If that's you, let's connect!
